# Intro to Certifications (certs) 

As a high school student who's interested in the cybersecurity field, I've learned over the past 2 years how prominent and important certifications are. Certifications are official credentials given by companies as a proven achievement of one's passing of an exam. Among the most common certifications are the CCNA (Cisco Certified Network Associate), the Security+ certification, and the A+ certification. But when it comes to industry certifications, there are many different ones with several content areas. Which ones you get depend on the area of study or position you are choosing. Today I will break down what I believe are the top 10 cybersecurity certifications to get in 2026 for high school students, recent graduates, and career changers. There are three major categories of difficulty, as some may have prior background knowledge to this. 

# 1. CompTIA Security+

Administrator: CompTIA (one of the most common admins)

Area of Study: Baseline cybersecurity skills + more in-depth security knowledge

Exam Format: Maximum 90 questions made of multiple-choice and performance-based questions, timed for 90 minutes

Costs: Depends on the retailer; directly from CompTIA is $439 for an exam voucher.

Passing: Out of 900 total points, 750 are required to pass (approximately 83.3%).

Benefits: The most recognized entry-level cybersecurity certification. Opens doors to SOC, IT support, and junior security roles. One of the best certifications for an entry-level individual looking for opportunity.

Difficulty: Beginner, definitely recommended for high school students.

# 2. ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity

Administrator: ISC2

Area of study: Security principles, incident response, business continuity, disaster recovery, access control basics, network security, and security operations

Exam Format: 2 hours for 100-125 multiple-choice questions.

Costs: $199 for the exam, plus a $50 Annual Maintenance Fee (AMF) once you pass.

Passing: Out of 1000 points, 700 is required to pass (70%). 

Benefits: Validates foundational knowledge in security principles, business continuity, access controls, network security, and incident response. It also serves as a stepping stone to more advanced certifications (hint: read to the bottom to see one). 

Difficulty: Beginner, highly recommended for career changers and those looking to get started in the field.

# 3. Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate

Administrator: Coursera + Google

Areas of study: Cybersecurity basics, risk management, networks and network security, Linux and SQL basics, identifying threats and vulnerabilities, classifying assets, Python scripting basics, and interview/job preparation with resume building and exam prep for the Security+ exam. 

Format: No exam for this certificate. It's an 8-9 module course on Coursera, filled with quizzes, hands-on labs, and assignments that are posted with peer review in order to obtain the credential. 

Costs: Requires a $50/month payment to Coursera; the timeline is typically 3-6 months for the certification.

Passing: Just going through and scoring well on all the material in order to complete the course and obtain the certificate. 

Benefits: Very strong for high school students, recent graduates, and career changers. Provides a wide range of skills that can be put on resumes, used for more advanced projects, and can even potentially be used for college credits. 

Difficulty: Beginner, requires no prior knowledge or experience, and is extremely valuable for anyone.

# 4. CompTIA Network+

Administrator: CompTIA

Area of study: Network connectivity, documentation, service configuration, data centers, cloud, virtual networking, monitoring, troubleshooting, and security hardening

Exam Format: 90 minutes, maximum 90 multiple-choice and performance-based questions

Costs: Varies by vendor; from CompTIA directly is $369. 

Passing: Out of 900 points, a minimum score of 720 is required to pass (80%). 

Benefits: Vendor-neutral skills are gained, foundational IT knowledge is gained, and it prepares you for jobs such as help desk or network support technician.

Difficulty: Beginner, no knowledge is required beforehand.

# 5. CompTIA A+

Administrator: CompTIA 

Area of Study: Foundational skills in hardware, software, networking, troubleshooting, and security. 

Exam Format: Split into two parts (Core 1 and Core 2), and takers are required to pass two separate 90-minute exams, each with approximately 80-83 questions. 

Costs: $530 total, $265 per voucher.

Passing: A 675 out of 900 points on the Core 1 exam and a 700 out of 900 points on the Core 2 exam. Total: 1375/1800 points required to pass. 

Benefits: Shows employers that you understand how the basics of operating systems, troubleshooting techniques, hardware/software management, and security methods work, which is excellent for someone with no IT background or new to the field. 

Difficulty: Beginner, no knowledge is required beforehand.

# 6. CompTIA CySA+

Administrator: CompTIA 

Area of study: Security operations, vulnerability management, incident response, reporting, and communication

Exam Format: A maximum of 85 questions made of multiple-choice, drag-and-drop, and performance-based questions timed for 2 hours and 45 minutes

Costs: Depends on the vendor voucher; directly from CompTIA+ is $439.

Passing: Out of 900 points, a 750 is the minimum required score to pass the exam.

Benefits: It's the industry standard for cybersecurity analytics and meets governmental standards in several countries for different companies, including the U.S. Department of Defense, Japan Banking Systems (JBS), and BlacKnight Cyber Security International. 

Difficulty: Intermediate; cybersecurity basics must be known before taking this exam.

# 7. Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) 

Administrator: Cisco Systems, Inc.

Area of study: Network fundamentals, network access, IP connectivity, IP services, security fundamentals, and automation/programmability.

Exam Format: 2 hours, varying between 80-120 questions consisting of multiple choice, drag-and-drop, performance-based labs, and simlets/testlets.

Costs: The exam is $300 plus taxes, but total study costs may be between $450 and $1000 depending on the cost of prep materials.

Passing: Depends on the difficulty of the specific set of questions you get, as Cisco uses a scaled 1000-1200 point scale, so approximately 825-850 points (75%-80%) should be enough to pass the exam. 

Benefits: Extremely strong for career growth, unmatched industry recognition, and an industry standard for upper-level networking roles.

Difficulty: Intermediate; networking basics are required to take and do well in this course.

# 8. CompTIA PenTest+ 

Administrator: CompTIA

Area of Study: Engagement management, reconnaissance and enumeration, vulnerability discovery and analysis, attacks and exploits, and post-exploitation and lateral movement. 

Exam format: 2 hours and 45 minutes for up to 90 multiple-choice and performance-based questions

Costs: Depends on the vendor voucher; directly it is $439.

Passing: Out of 900 points, a score of 750 points is required to pass.

Benefits: Meets government standards for the US, opens a wide range of career fields, has tremendous real-world application, and is the industry standard for penetration roles and positions in cybersecurity.

Difficulty: Intermediate; many gain foundational certifications such as Network+ or Security+ before going for this certification. 

# 9. Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP)

Administrator: OffSec

Area of Study: Active Directory (30%) and Standalone Targets (70%)

Exam Format: Strict performance-based test, 24-hour practical exam. Candidates are placed in a simulated lab network and must successfully find vulnerabilities, exploit systems, escalate privileges, and write a professional penetration testing report to pass.

Costs: The entire bundle, which includes the PEN-200 course material, 90 days of lab access, and one exam attempt, is $1749, with additional retakes costing $249.

Passing: Must achieve a 70 out of 100 point score plus a successful penetration testing report.

Benefits: High salaries among holders, strong writing skills to show employers, unlocks pentesting roles, lifetime validation, and is the gold standard for pentesters.

Difficulty: Advanced. A solid foundation in Linux administration, Windows environments, networking protocols, and basic scripting/programming (Python and Bash) is required to effectively do well on this certification. 

# 10. Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)	

Administrator: ISC2

Area of Study: 8 set domains: Security and Risk Management, Asset Security, Security Architecture and Engineering, Communication and Network Security, Identity and Access Management (IAM), Security Assessment and Testing, Security Operations, and Software Development Security. 

Exam Format: Uses Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT) for English exams with 3 hours for 100-150 questions (multiple-choice, drag-and-drop, and hotspot). For non-English exams, you will see a linear, fixed-form format with 250 questions and a 6-hour time limit.

Costs: The exam fee is $749; $998 is the price for the "Peace of Mind" exam voucher that includes a free retake, and to keep it, you must pay a $135 AMF fee. None of this includes prep materials, rescheduling exam dates ($50 per), or cancellations ($100 per), so total costs will range between about $950 and $4500.

Passing: Out of 1000 points, a minimum score of 700 is required to pass. 

Benefits: High salary potential, leadership advancement, global credibility (recognized in 170+ countries), and compliance with governmental requirements.

Difficulty: Advanced, by far one of the hardest certifications to obtain in the field. Requires 5+ years of experience across the 8 tested domains. 

# What to Do After Getting a Certification?
Employers don't look at only certifications. They want to know you can apply what you know into real-world settings. In cybersecurity, you learn content for the goal of knowing what you're doing and what you're dealing with. But you won't ever get perfect scenarios where everything aligns, so it's important to start projects to apply what you've learned. 

After earning a certification, the best next step is to start building hands-on experience. This can include creating a home lab, participating in Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions, completing cybersecurity challenges, contributing to projects, or practicing with platforms like TryHackMe and Hack The Box. Me personally, I do CTFs and I'm currently working to build some Linux virtual machines and Cisco Packet Tracer labs for my school's cybersecurity club. Do what works for you. Recommended pathway: Basic certification --> hands-on-projects --> choose specialization --> job search.

Many who gain these certifications aren't even networking/cybersecurity students; they're looking to enter the field. This next step is absolutely crucial for them. That way, you can show something physical to employers and begin choosing a specialization, such as incident response or cloud security. Slowly, but surely, you will build your way to a top role in the field.

Thank you for reading and good luck on any future journeys!
